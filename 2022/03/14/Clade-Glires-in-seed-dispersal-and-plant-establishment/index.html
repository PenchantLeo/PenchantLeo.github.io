Environmental factors & Biotic interactions

Soil properties

Olfaction is relavent to the humidity of the environment include the air and the soil. In the humid zone seeds are more likely to be found. In the arid zone (include burnt area) the seeds tend to be harder to be found.

Habitat Structure and Predation Risk

Shrubs and tall-growing grasses and forbs can provide a shelter for the predators to handle the seeds.

In open habitat or vegetation gaps the seeds are negligible or happen more hastily. Which has been proved in YJA. (Dali, Yunnan Province, China)

Ungulates also influence the small rodents predation. Ungulates like hog, could compete with rodents. Its appearance lowes the survival rate of the seeds.

Rodents’ pilfering-avoiding mechanism may be a driver of the evolution of seed traits.

Biotic Interactions

The oak with medium-sized acorns indirectly promoted seedling establishment of the one with large acorns, but hindered seed-seedling transitions in the oak with small acorns

Seeds with no or short dormancy period can support the survival of seeds with long dormancy period as dormant seeds are less attractive for immediate consumption but are preferred for long-term storing.

there may be remarkable differences in the seed dispersal effectiveness of small rodents in certain habitats with variable proportion of the seeds of palatable plant species.

Threat factors

Habitat Loss & Degradation

Due to different Reactions made by rodents and plants when facing the fragmentation, the interactions between rodents and plants are different.

And seeds are seldomly moved across roads(Happened in YJA) and remained in the fragmented stands so that long-distance dispersal was hindered.
Indicating that roads functioned as impenetrable barriers for hoarding

Some plant species show adaptive responses to fragmentation, which also affect the interactions.

Alien Species

Alien Plants

The more attractive seeds of introduced and cultivated plant species can alter animals’ preference and divide their attention and energy investment, which may result in a decreased dispersal rate of native plants and may support the colonization of non-native plants

In contrast, in case of the co-occurrence of the native sessile oak (Q. petraea) and the alien red oak, field mice (Apodemus sp.) preferentially caches the acorns of the native species.

The contradicting results may be explained by the differences in the studied seed-dispersing animal taxa as seed preference and handling can vary greatly among species

The ratio of native and alien oak species could also affect the caching rate of acorns.

Alien Rodents

The spread of alien small rodent and lagomorph species could also considerably change seed removal rate, seed availability as well as seed and seedling survival

rats destroyed more seeds by chewing and did not scarify seeds, which resulted in lower rates of germination and elongated germination time for the surviving seeds,

Gut passage since the hard seed coat is partially digested which improves the ratio of germinated seeds and the speed germination.

In return, ice plants provide a water- and nutrient-rich food source for the rats during the dry season.
