The transformation of my perspective of Seed dispersal

In this article, Vander Wall raises that seed fate sometimes not that bright and clear like we think.
And a lot researches is not that convincable due to lack of solid evidences.

Issues pointed out by Wall

In fact, the processes of seed dispersal is very complex. Many factors in the environment are get invloved. Include abiotic factors and animal factors.
Many situations contain Secondary Dispersal.
There are so many possibilities in the postdispersal seeds removal. Including predations and multisteps seed dispersal processes.

Vander Wall used 1/3 length of the article to discuss the possibility that Multisteps seeds dispersal exists.
Including give the example of shit-beetles (Scarabaeine) who plays a big role in this process.

So many existing researches assume that removed seeds are eaten by predators which is unwarranted. He thinks so base on 2 reasons:

  • unreliable knowledges accumulated and researchers get misled.
  • Assuming that removed seeds are dead causes researchers to miss potentially important events in the seed dispersal process.

About the process of seed dispersal of Fagaceae

  • Blate et al. (1998) monitored the removal of 40 species of seeds in Kalimantan, Indonesia. Among these species was Lithocarpus sp. (Fagaceae), which produces large (8.1-g) acorns. Lithocarpus, like most other members of this large family, is known to be dispersed by seed-caching rodents and corvids(Vander wall).
  • Mature acorns fall to the ground and removal is an essential step in the dispersal process; acorns that are not quickly removed die or are eaten by animals that offer no prospect of dispersal.
  • Hau (1997) monitored the removal of Quercus, Lithocarpus, and Castanopsis (Fagaceae) nuts on degraded hillsides in Hong Kong, China, by rodents (very likely Niviventer and Rattus) and considered removal to be seed predation.
  • Zhang et al. (2004) report that Niviventer and Rattus are important agents of nut dispersal at several sites in China.

What should we do in the Lithocarpus researches

  1. If we hope to understand the details of plant recruitment.
    we need to determine how often seeds escape predation, how this occurs, and what are the fates of these seeds.

  2. Even though is so hard to tack the seeds. But following seeds is the only sure way to gain reliable knowledge of seed fates

Enlightenment

Wall didn’t bring up any method that can efficiently track the seeds so it’s an blank space to the seed dispersal researches.
