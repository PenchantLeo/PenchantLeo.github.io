Summary

This article determines whether seed predation was a barrier to natural forest regeneration on degraded hillsides.

Vander Wall tends to recognise that verbrates predation is a double edge sword and the benefits are bigger than those losses.

This is already varified in Wall’s paper that seed predation and dispersal is good for plants’ seeds’ spread.

Because frugivores and herbivores would spead the seeds and some of them hoard them underground.

So I’ll focus on the Result and Methods of the research.

High rodent densities in Hong Kong may be due to low predator populations and high ground cover of shrubland and grassland.

Its enemies’ reduction also play a big part in the process. (The natural enemies reduced sharply by traping, habitat destruction and fragmentation. )

It seems like the mass of the seed is not that matter with wether predators eat or not.

Conclusion

Seed predation is a signiﬁcant barrier to natural forest regeneration in degraded hillsides in Hong Kong.
